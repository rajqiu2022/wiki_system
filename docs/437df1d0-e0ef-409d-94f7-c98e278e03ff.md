# 4G LTE Air Monitor

## 1. Introduction

This air monitor, with the detection of air temperature and humidity, carbon dioxide, air quality TVOC, light intensity and other functions, mainly used in greenhouse environmental detection.

Its built-in 4G module, can transmit temperature, humidity, light intensity, carbon dioxide concentration and TVOC to the Internet to achieve remote wireless communication.

This air monitor is powered by internal 18650 lipo battery charged by solar panel, the power down current lower as 165uA. With the solar panel to charge the battery and low power consumption, the device can operate for extended periods. And then transmit the data via the 4G LTE network, so user can monitor the field status or have them recorded for long-term analyze.

Model:[4GLAMESP32S3](https://www.makerfabs.com/4g-lte-air-monitor-air-temperature-humidity-light-co2.html)

## 2. Features

- Controller: ESP32-S3-WROOM-1, PCB Antenna, 16MB Flash, 8MB PSRAM, ESP32-S3-WROOM-1-N16R8
- Wireless: WiFi, Bluetooth 5.0 & 4G LTE CAT1
- Global used LTE Cat-1, SIM7670G with uplink rate 5 Mbps and downlink rate 10 Mbps
- GNSS Positioning
- MPPT: CN3791 solar maximum power tracking charging management
- BMS: DW06 over-charge, over-discharge protection
- Solar Power supply connector: 4.5~28V

## 6. MQTTS Demo

This demo shows how to use MQTTS. An ESP32-S3 board transfers data to MQTT broker via WiFi and received by 4G LTE Air Monitor via 4G.
![MQTT_Diagram_V1.0_P1B.jpg](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2025-06-19_17_20_56_0.jpg)
1. Install the Adafruit MQTT library from library manager. What we used is v2.5.9.
![MQTT library20250619164803.jpg](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2025-06-19_16_49_13_0.jpg)
2. Open the [mqtt\_secure\_esp32s3](https://github.com/Makerfabs/4G-LTE-CAT1-Air-Monitor/tree/main/example/MQTTS_Demo/mqtt_secure_esp32s3) demo by Arduino IDE, modify the WiFi to your local network.

```
#define WLAN_SSID "Makerfabs"
#define WLAN_PASS "20160704"
```

1. Compile and upload to a ESP32-S3 board. Now we can put it aside.
2. Download the CA certification to SIM7670G (Refer to SIM7672X & SIM7652X\_Series\_AT\_Command\_Manual\_V1.00.pdf).
   ![MQTT20250619A.jpg](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2025-06-19_16_58_45_0.jpg)
3. Open the [4G\_LTE\_MQTTS](https://github.com/Makerfabs/4G-LTE-CAT1-Air-Monitor/tree/main/example/MQTTS_Demo/4G_LTE_MQTTS_V1_0) demo by Arduino IDE, compile and upload to 4G LTE Air Monitor.
