# SenseCAP M2 Multi-Platform LoRaWAN Indoor Gateway

## 1. Gateway Network Configuration

Connect the antenna and power adaptor to the gateway.
The power LED will show in red, and in about 15s, the indicator on the top will flash green, indicating that the gateway is booting.

![1.jpg](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_51_57_0.jpg)

## 2. Connect to the Internet

There are two ways to connect to the Internet. Choose the one that works for you.

### 2.1 Connect to Ethernet Cable

Connect the Ethernet cable to the Ethernet port, and the indicator on the top will show solid green if the gateway is successfully connected to the internet.

### 2.2 Connect to WIFI via Luci

There are two ways for users to login Luci configuration page.

- Access via router.
- Access via device AP hotspot.

#### 2.2.1 Access via router

- Step 1: Connect your device to a Ethernet cable, and connect your PC to the same router.

**Note:** Your PC and device should be connected to the same router/network.

- Step 2: Get IP Address of your device
  Check the IP Address of your device on your router admin page.
- Step 3: Get your device Username and Password,
  You can find the Username and Password on your device label.

![2.jpg](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_52_16_0.jpg)

- Step 4: Login Luci
  Input the IP Address of your device in a browser to enter the Luci page.

Then input your device username and password to login.

![image3.jpg](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_52_33_0.jpg)

#### 2.2.2 Access via device AP hotspot

- Step 1: Turn on the device AP hotspot.

Press the button for 5s until the blue indicator flashes slowly to enter the configuration mode.

- Step 2: Connect to the AP hotspot.

AP hotspot name is SenseCAP\_XXXXXX (6-figure MAC address),default password is 12345678; connect your computer to this AP hotspot.

- Step 3: Log in to the Local Console.

Input the IP Address (192.168.168.1) in your browser to enter the Local Console. Then input your device username and password, and click the Login button.

**Note:**You can find the Username and Password on your device label.

![image3.jpg](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_53_02_0.jpg)

#### 2.2.3 Connect to a WIFI

- Click on "Network --> Wireless".

![image5.jpg](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_53_44_0.jpg)

- Click on the **Scan** button to scan the WIFI.

![image6.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_55_00_0.png)

- Select your WI-FI to join the network.

![image7.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_55_14_0.png)

- Submit the Wi-Fi password, and then click **Submit** and **Save**.

![image8.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_55_24_0.png)

Then click Save and Apply to apply your settings.

The indicator on the top will show solid green if the gateway is successfully connected to the WIFI.

### 3.2 Connect Gateway to The Things Network

There are two ways to connect to the Things Network:

- Packet forward.

Semtech UDP Packet Forwarder is the original LoRaWANÂ® packet forwarder, connecting to servers through the Semtech UDP protocol.

- Basics Station.

LoRa Basics Station is the preferred way of connecting Gateways to The Things Stack.

Choose a way to connect your gateway.

#### 3.2.1 Connecting via Packet Forwarder

##### TTN Configuration

- Step 1:Log into The Things Stack.

If you don't have a TTN account, please register first.

![ttnlogin in.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_55_57_0.png)

- Step 2: Register the gateway.

![register gateway.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_56_14_0.png)

Enter your Gateway EUI and click "confirm".

**Gateway EUI:** Gateway EUI can be found on the device label or Local Console.

![enter EUI.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_56_34_0.png)

Enter your Gateway ID, Gateway ID, Frequency plan and click "Register gateway".

**Gateway ID:** A unique identifier for your gateway(the ID must contain only lowercase letters, numbers, and dashes).

**Gateway name:** A name of your gateway.

**Frequency plan:** Select the corresponding frequency according to your gateway version.

![enter id.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_57_00_0.png)

You can check the Gateway in the overview after successful registration.

##### Gateway Configuration

Configure the gateway via the Web UI, please to log into Luci page first.

- Step 1: LoRa Network Settings

Navigate to LoRa --> LoRa Network.

![lora.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_57_15_0.png)

- Step 2: Set Mode to Packet Forward.

**Gateway EUI:** It will automatically get the EUI of the connected gateway.

**Server Address:** For Semtech UDP Packet Forwarder use .
The  is the address of your The Things Stack deployment.
See Server Addresses for more info.

**Server Port(Up/Down):** The Up Port and Down Port are typically 1700.
Other settings can be left as default, or can be changed to suit your requirements.

Click "Save&Apply" to apply your settings.

![packet.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_57_32_0.png)

- Step 3: Channel Plan Settings

Navigate to LoRa --> Channel Plan.

![channel plan.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_57_50_0.png)

Select the Region and Frequency plan according to the actual choice.

After setting, click Save&Apply.

![region.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_58_09_0.png)

#### 3.2.2 Connecting via Basic Station

##### TTN Configuration

- Step 1: Register gateway

![register gateway.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_58_46_0.png)

![enter EUI.png](https://www.makerfabs.com/image/wiki_image/2024-11-17_16_58_57_0.png)

This will only allow a gateway to connect if it uses a TLS enabled Basic Station or MQTT connection.
